The Navigation Challenge

Why robots get lost

Imagine a robot placed in an unknown environment with no GPS. If it relies only on its wheel sensors (Dead Reckoning), minor wheel slips compound over time, causing its position estimate to drift rapidly into error.

Actual Path
Dead Reckoning (Drifting)

The Solution: SLAM

Simultaneous Localization and Mapping

To navigate accurately, the robot needs a map. But to build a map, it needs to know its exact location.

This chicken-and-egg problem is called SLAM.

EKF-SLAM solves this by estimating the robot's pose and the coordinates of surrounding landmarks at the exact same time, using a single unified filter.

The Mathematics

1. The State Vector ($x$)

The Extended Kalman Filter (EKF) maintains a combined State Vector that tracks everything we care about.

It contains the robot's position ($x,y$), heading ($\theta$), and the coordinates of all discovered landmarks ($m_i$).

The Mathematics

2. The Covariance Matrix ($P$)

The filter also maintains a Covariance Matrix representing the system's uncertainty.

It tracks the uncertainty and correlation of every estimate. As landmarks are observed repeatedly, their cross-correlations strengthen, locking down the map.

The Filter Cycle

Predict and Update

The EKF rapidly repeats two phases to estimate the world in real-time:

1. Predict

Projects movement using velocity inputs, adding motion uncertainty to the state.

2. Update

Corrects the estimate using sensor measurements of nearby landmarks.
